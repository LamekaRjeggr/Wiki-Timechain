Add to the record

This page reads the record. The only things it ever writes are what you tap out yourself — a reaction, or a margin comment once you've reacted, each signed by your own key. It never writes a card, and it never asks for your secret key. The timelines live on public nostr relays, so contributing happens from any nostr client that can publish wiki events — you don't need permission from this site, and this site can't stop you. That's the point.

1 · Get a nostr identity

A nostr account is just a keypair — no signup, no email. Start at nstart.me or read nostr.how for a walkthrough. Guard your secret key (nsec) — it can't be reset. Use a signer extension or app rather than pasting it into websites.

2 · Publish a card

An entry is a kind 30818 wiki event (NIP-54). Wiki clients such as wikifreedia.xyz or wikistr.com can publish them. A card that this page can place on a timeline carries these tags:

kind: 30818
tags:
  d           2026-my-entry-slug          (identity — one entry, editable by you)
  title       2026 · Short entry name
  event_date  2026-07-20                  (YYYY-MM-DD — where it sits on the rail)
  c           <collection>                (which timeline — a NEW value starts a new one)
content:
  One factual sentence about what happened.

  Source: [label](https://link-to-primary-source)

Keep the content to verifiable facts with a primary source. The timeline shows who published every card — your entry stands on your signature. There is no registration step for a timeline: this page discovers collections from the cards themselves (any card with an event_date and a c tag), so publishing the first card of a new c value is all it takes.

3 · Dispute a card

Disagree with an entry? Publish your own kind-30818 with the same d tag and your correction. Nothing is overwritten — both versions appear side by side on the rail, each signed by its author, and readers judge for themselves. Disputes are additions, not deletions.

Verify everything

Every card links to its author — click a publisher's name to inspect their full history on njump.me. This site has no accounts, no moderators, and no server: it renders whatever the relays hold, and the relays hold whatever people sign.
